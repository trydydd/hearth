# CafeBox Chat — Privacy Notice

This document explains how your chat messages are handled on this CafeBox.

## What is stored

When you send a message in the chat, it is stored on the box until the
operator clears it. Messages are **not** automatically deleted when you
disconnect or when the box is switched off.

Assume that messages persist for the entire time this box is in operation,
unless the operator tells you otherwise.

## Encryption

If you turn on encryption in Element (the chat app), your messages are
scrambled before they leave your device. The box stores the scrambled
version but **cannot read it** — only devices with your encryption keys can
decode it.

If you do **not** turn on encryption, your messages are stored as plain
text. Anyone with access to the box could read them.

Encryption is turned on by default when you create a new room. You can check
whether a room is encrypted by looking for the padlock icon in Element.

## Who can see your messages

- **Without encryption:** the operator of this box, and anyone else with
  physical access to it.
- **With encryption:** only people in the room who have your encryption keys.

## No internet connection

This chat is completely offline. Messages never leave the local network and
are never sent to any external server.

## Recommendation

Enable encryption before sharing anything sensitive. If in doubt, assume
your messages may be visible to the operator of this box.
